Strength Guidelines
How to Adapt Strength Through Phases
Base Phase (Mar–Apr)
Full 2× sessions. Maintain strength gains. Heavy-ish loads on pull-ups and press (5–8 rep range). Lunges and BSS for lower body.
Elevation Phase (May–Jun)
Drop to 1× session per week (Session A). Reduce load ~20%. Keep all your key upper body movements. Prioritize single-leg calf work.
Peak & Taper
1× light maintenance (peak), 0–1× taper week 1, zero week before race. Focus is freshness. Muscle memory is established.
🎯
Your Physique Goals — Respected
Shoulder press, pull-ups, and lateral raises are permanent fixtures in both sessions throughout the plan. You won't lose your upper body development — the volume and structure preserves it. What changes is the lower body: BSS and lunges replace any barbell squatting to build trail-specific unilateral strength without the CNS load of heavy bilateral lifts.
Fueling Strategy

NUTRITION & FUEL

Race nutrition is a skill. Practice it every long run from 18km onwards.

Pre-Run Protocol
Before Long Runs & Race
Race morning: Wake up 3h before start. Same breakfast you've practiced. No new foods. Coffee if you normally drink it.
During the Run
Fueling on the Move
Fuel Types
What to Carry & Use
🧃
Energy Gels
22–25g carbs each. Easy to carry. Practice your brand — GI issues are real.
🍌
Real Food
Bananas, rice balls, dates — aid stations will have options. Essential for 51km.
🥤
Sports Drink
Carbs + electrolytes in one. Use in vest soft flask alongside plain water.
🍫
Chews / Bars
Variety prevents flavor fatigue after 4+ hours. Pack 2–3 types minimum.
🧂
Electrolytes
Sodium critical for 8–12h effort. SaltStick or similar every 60–90 min.
🍵
Broth (Aid Stations)
Warm, salty, sodium-rich. Your stomach may crave savory after hours of sweet.
Recovery Nutrition
After Long Runs
Within 30 min Recovery shake or chocolate milk + banana 3:1 carb:protein
1–2 hours after Full meal: rice or pasta, lean protein, vegetables High carb priority
Day after Extra carb-rich meals + 8–9h sleep priority Glycogen rebuild
Daily carb needs for heavy training: 6–8g per kg bodyweight = ~470–620g carbs/day on high-volume weeks. Don't under-eat — you're burning thousands of calories on long run days.
